§ I — Inquiry

The work, in brief.

My research sits at the intersection of compressed sensing, sparse modeling, and deep learning, applied to the high-dimensional signal that perceptual and biological systems contend with. The recurring question: what is the minimal structure that supports inference, and how do learned priors recover what the senses can only sample?

I joined Florida Atlantic University's Center for Complex Systems and Brain Sciences in 2011 and completed my Ph.D. in 2016. In 2014 I co-founded the Machine Perception and Cognitive Robotics Laboratory — a group of scientists, engineers, and artists building novel hardware and software for embodied intelligence.

Note i.
"Sparse" is meant literally — most real signals are well-approximated by few active components, if you know where to look.
Note ii.
The lab is deliberately cross-disciplinary; engineering alone has never been enough.
