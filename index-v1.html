01 — About

Background

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Florida Atlantic University. My research sits at the meeting point of compressed sensing, sparse modeling, and deep learning — with applications spanning computer vision, computational neuroscience, drug discovery, and autonomous systems.

I earned my B.S. in Physics and Mathematics from Guilford College in 2008, working on neural networks and swarm optimization. I then studied artificial intelligence and immune systems at the University of North Carolina at Greensboro before joining FAU's Center for Complex Systems and Brain Sciences in 2011, where I completed my Ph.D. in 2016 on sparse coding and compressed sensing.

In 2014 I co-founded the Machine Perception and Cognitive Robotics Laboratory to build a team of scientists, programmers, and engineers exploring novel hardware and software environments for embodied intelligence.

02 — Research

Areas of work

Methods built on sparse representations and learned priors, applied to the messy, high-dimensional signal that real perceptual and biological systems contend with.

Compressed Sensing

Reconstruction from undersampled signals; sparsity-driven inference.

Sparse Modeling

Dictionary learning and sparse coding for natural and biological signals.

Deep Learning

Transformer and CNN architectures for perception, prediction, and discovery.

Computer Vision

Pattern recognition, feature extraction, classification at scale.

Computational Neuroscience

Models of cortical computation and biologically-inspired learning.

Applied ML

Drug discovery, telesurgery, substance-use surveillance, financial prediction.
